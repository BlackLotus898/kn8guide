🚀 Getting Started // Day 1 ▼

When you first launch the game, you'll be walked through an introductory battle and given the chance to select a free ★5 character. This choice matters — pick wisely based on the playstyle you want to build around.

What to Do First
  • Select your free ★5 — Attackers are recommended for beginners as they clear content fastest.
  • Link your account — Connect a Google, Apple, or social login immediately to protect your progress.
  • Start the Main Story — Follow the main quest chain to unlock features and earn Dimensional Crystals.
  • Claim all login rewards — Check the inbox and event tab for free resources waiting to be claimed.
Reroll tip: If you're unhappy with your free ★5 selection or your early pulls, new accounts can reroll by clearing app data and starting fresh. This is most worthwhile if you're targeting a specific top-tier character.

📈 Progression & Leveling // Building Up ▼

Character progression in K8 The Game involves leveling, skill upgrades, weapon equipping, uniparts, and ascension nodes. Focus on your main squad first before branching out.

Character Leveling Priority
  • Level a few characters from each role — teams always need to be changed to fit the needs of the battle. Don't just focus on one main Attacker.
  • Having a leveled Attacker, Defender, and Supporter from the start gives you flexibility across all content types.
  • Use EXP materials only on characters you actively use — don't waste them on bench units early.
Skill Upgrades
  • Check each character's Skill Upgrade Priority on their character page before spending materials.
  • Generally, Passive Skills and Combat Skills provide the most value early.
  • Don't spread skill materials across every character — max out your core squad first.
Ascension Nodes
  • Ascension (A1–A6) significantly boosts a character's power. Prioritize A1 and A2 nodes first as they provide the strongest bonuses.
  • A3 and A5 are Skill Level nodes — unlock these when you have materials to immediately level those skills.
Unlocked Potential is a post-max enhancement system available for select characters. Once a character is fully maxed, this allows further skill and stat upgrades. Currently available for Isao Shinomiya (The Unbreakable Iron Wall), Mina Ashiro (Off the Scale), and Soshiro Hoshina (Hoshina-Style Sword-Slay Technique).

Attribute Matching
  • Match at least 2 characters' attack types to the Kaiju's weakness for maximum damage.
  • Check the Kaiju's weakness before a battle and swap squad members accordingly.
  • Elemental types (Fire, Ice, Wind, Lightning, Water) deal bonus damage to specific Kaiju.
Synergy Tips
  • Battle Buddy system — some characters have skills that specifically buff or interact with their Battle Buddy (the character positioned next to them). Check character pages for buddy-specific skills.
  • Supporters like Chester Lochburn and Haruichi Izumo provide universal buffs that boost the whole squad.
  • Pair DoT characters (Burn, Shock, Windbite) together to maximize damage-over-time output.
💎 Gacha & Pulling // Crystals ▼

Dimensional Crystals are the main gacha currency. Spend them wisely — limited characters return in reruns but good opportunities are still worth saving for.

How to Earn Crystals for Free
  • Story Modes — Main Story, Anime Story, and Memory Stories each award Crystals on first clear.
  • Daily missions — Log in and complete daily tasks every day for a steady trickle.
  • Events — Kaiju Rush, Fierce Battle Area, and limited events are some of the best sources.
  • Player Rank rewards — Each rank-up grants a Crystal bonus.
Smart Pulling Tips
  • Use the Web Shop — Always purchase Crystals from the official web store, not in-game. You get significantly more for the same price.
  • Understand the pity system — Pulls accumulate toward a guaranteed ★5.
  • Save for limited banners — Featured characters on rate-up banners have increased odds. General pool characters appear at any time.
  • Don't impulse pull — If you don't have a specific target, save your Crystals.

⚔️ Combat Basics // Fighting Kaiju ▼

K8 The Game uses a turn-based combat system centered around exposing and exploiting the Kaiju's core. Understanding these fundamentals will carry you through all content.

The Core System
  • Every Kaiju has a hidden core. Use skills that match the Kaiju's weakness attribute to expose it.
  • When the core is exposed, the Kaiju takes significantly increased damage.
  • Save your Ultimate attacks for when the core is exposed — this is when they deal full damage.
  • The core exposure window is limited — coordinate your squad's turn order to maximize damage during it.
SP (Skill Points)
  • SP is shared across your squad and spent on Combat Skills.
  • Basic Normal Attacks are free — skills cost SP.
  • Manage SP carefully — don't burn it all early if you'll need it for a key skill later in the fight.
  • Some characters and skills generate SP or reduce costs — check character pages for details.
PLT (Plates)
  • Kaiju have PLT (Plates) that must be destroyed to fully expose the core.
  • Certain attack types are more effective at destroying specific PLT types.
  • Destroying PLT also charges your Ultimate Gauge, so Normal Attacks on PLT are never wasted.
For more detail on elements, damage types, ailments, debuffs, and advanced combat mechanics — check the Combat Guide.
🔧 Weapons & Uniparts // Equipment ▼

Equipping the right weapon and uniparts can dramatically boost a character's performance. Always check the recommended loadout on each character's page.

Weapons
  • Each character uses a specific weapon type — they cannot equip weapons outside their type.
  • ★5 weapons provide the strongest bonuses. Prioritize getting the recommended weapon for your main Attacker first.
  • Check the Weapons & Best Users page for a full list of who uses what.
  • Exception: Kaiju No. 8 variants cannot equip weapons — their power comes entirely from skills and ascension.
Uniparts
  • Uniparts are equipment pieces that provide stat bonuses based on set effects.
  • Follow the Stat Priority listed on each character's page when choosing uniparts (e.g. ATK > CRIT DMG > SPD).
  • Set bonuses activate when you equip multiple pieces of the same type — aim for full sets on your main units.
